Public Health Act of 1848
A serious outbreak of cholera prompted British government to finally take action and fix the problem
It allowed towns to:
1. Set up their own local Board of Health
2. Appoint a local medical officer
3. Organize the removal of rubbish
4. Build a working sewer system

New Cholera Outbreak in 1866
Since it was after new advances in better hygiene, towns enforcing the new laws were least affected.

1870's
Joseph Chamberlain, the mayor of Birmingham took action
to reduce rubbish and disease by demolishing over 40 acres of slums

Public Health Act of 1875
Local authorities responsible for:
1. Clean water
2. Public toilets
3. Rubbish removal
4. Sewers and drains

The Great Stink of 1858
Plans for a new sewer system in London were being made
Extremely hot weather meant Thames River level was VERY low,
exposing the sewage along its banks causing a horrible stench
Parliament was not able to meet due to the conditions
